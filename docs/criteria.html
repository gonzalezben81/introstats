Rubric for Grading Group Presentations

Criteria Ratings Pts
Teamwork Excellent 3
The group worked very well with each other, and the presentation was shared equally among the group members.
Meets Expectations 2
The group worked well with each other and communicated well. Some members participated slightly more than others.
Approaches Expectations 1
Group communicated relatively well with a few lapses in the presentation; some students dominated the presentation and others did not participate much.
Needs Improvement 0
Group did not work well together. There were obvious miscommunications and lapses in the presentation.
Focus Excellent 3
The thesis is very clearly stated; the topic is narrowed sufficiently; the specific audience has clearly been taken into account when delivering the speech.
Meets Expectations 2
The thesis is clear or clearly implicit; it is implied that the specific audience has been considered when delivering the speech.
Approaches Expectations 1
The thesis is unclear; the topic is only partially limited; the specific audience has been partially considered when delivering the speech.
Needs Improvement 0
The topic and thesis are unclear; no apparent attempt has been made to limit the topic; the specific audience has not been considered.
Organization Excellent 3
The speech has a clear introduction that catches the audienceâ€™s attention effectively and is connected to the whole; effective transitions recap each main point; the conclusion effectively summarizes the speech and is related to the whole.
Meets Expectations 2
The introduction and conclusion are clear and somewhat related to the whole; some transitions are used.
Approaches Expectations 1
The introduction and conclusion may be ineffective and not related to the whole; the logical plan must be inferred, as no transitions are used.
Needs Improvement 0
The topic and thesis are unclear; no apparent attempt has been made to limit the topic; the specific audience has not been considered.
Development Excellent 3
All main points begin with a clear topic sentence; all main and supporting points are supported by specific and highly effective examples/evidence; the main and supporting points all relate to each other.
Meets Expectations 2
Most main points begin with a clear topic sentence; most main and supporting points include specific evidence/examples; most main and supporting points relate to each other.
Approaches Expectations 1
Some main points have stated or implied topic sentences; some main points are supported by specific evidence/examples; some main and supporting points relate to each other.
Needs Improvement 0
Main points contain no topic sentences; main points are not supported by specific examples/evidence; little or no supporting material is used; main and supporting points do not relate to each other.
Style Excellent 3
Language is memorable; language is well-chosen; tone is appropriate. Visuals and/or interactions with audience are clear, engaging, and fully support the presentation.
Meets Expectations 2
Most language is somewhat memorable; language usage is correct; tone is usually appropriate. Visuals and/or interactions with audience are mostly clear, engaging, and support the presentation.
Approaches Expectations 1
Language is not memorable; language usage is at times inaccurate; tone is at times inappropriate. Visuals and/or interactions with audience are somewhat clear, engaging, and support the presentation.
Needs Improvement 0
Language is not memorable or is confusing; language usage is often inaccurate; tone is inappropriate or distracting. Visuals and/or interactions with audience are not clear, engaging, or supporting of the presentation.
Requirements Excellent 3
Meets all requirements fully.
Approaches Expectations 1
Meets only some of the requirements.
Needs Improvement 0
Does not meet most/all of the requirements.

Total Points: 18
